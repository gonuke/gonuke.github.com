The Old Town Square

> Old Town Square
Rivalling the Charles Bridge as Prague's most well-known tourist attraction is the astronomical clock in the Old town square. The old town square is one of the most open areas we saw in Prague. In addition to a large statue in the middle of the square (with steps used by many to give their feet a rest), it is surrounded by a snapshot of the architecture seen around the rest of Prague's streets.

The Tyn Church whose spires are one of the sights of the Old Town Square.
The House at Stone Bell with its ornate facade and crown of statues.
