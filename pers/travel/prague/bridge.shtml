The Charles Bridge

> Charles Bridge
One of the most famous attractions of Prague is the Charles Bridge. On a nice day, you can spend nearly the whole day on this bridge, listening the to the street performers (such as the Klezmer type musicians to the right), browsing the artisans tables, and watching the people stream back and forth.

The bridge itself is lined with statues, has an arch at both ends, and offers some wondeful view back towards the banks. Throughout Prague you can find beautiful black and white photographs of a quiet version of Charles Bridge full of nothing but fog. In these photos (which you will never be able to take yourself thanks to all the people!) the statues loom magnificently up out of the fog and the bridge takes on an entirely different character.

On the west side of the river, I would encourage you to go down below the bridge where there is an entire neighborhood which appears to be less crowded by tourists than some of the other places in Prague.
