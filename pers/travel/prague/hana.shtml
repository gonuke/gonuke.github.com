Friends in Prague

> Friends
When he lived in Czechoslovakia, my grandfather had a good friend whose son and wife now live in Prague. The Green clan has been continuing this friendship to the next generation: my cousin and sister visited two years ago and my aunt and uncle just before that. We went there for dinner and had a wonderful evening. Although only Hana spoke english, Lubos could understand enough to follow the conversation. The most interesting food of our trip was that evening: blueberry filled dumplings with blueberry yogurt on top -- sweet and heavy and DELICIOUS! Hana then took us on a late night tour of Prague, along the river and up behind/above the castle.

We met Hana on Wenceslas Square, just in front of the National Museum of the Czech republic, under the statue of Wenceslas himself. The long square (more like a wide avenue with grass boulevard) slopes down away from the Museum towards the middle of town. It was awesome to stand at the top look down and imagine the whole square full of people as it was during the recent revolution.
