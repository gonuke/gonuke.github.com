Lifeforce Mill

Cavan Town, Co. Cavan

> Cavan Mill

The old mill in Cavan was recently bought by Lifeforce, a natural food producer, and restored to its original working condition. Although it was originally used to mill oats, because they were the local crop, it is now used to mill wheat for making bread. In fact, Lifeforce sells both the whole-meal flour and the whole-meal bread which it bakes with that flour.

One of the attractions of the Cavan Lifeforce Mill is a bread-making tour. The tour starts upstairs in a big kitchen where each visitor gets to mix their own bread batter following a standard Irish whole-meal soda bread recipe. Once all the batter has been mixed, it is put in loaf pans and taken to the ovens. Then, while the bread bakes, the visitors get to tour the entire mill from where the grain is delivered, through the storage and drying areas, and into the milling area itself.

Rather than a water wheel, this mill is powered by a water turbine, and is the oldest operating water turbine mill in Ireland. In case of low water flow, the back up system is a big old deisel engine. This has been supplemented even further with an electric back-up system which, due to low rainfall powers the mill most of the time. Being the geeky engineer that I am, I was fascinated by the mill wheels, the water trubine and the old deisel engine. The deisel engine was particularly interesting because of a laboratory course which I had in Toronto. This so-called "Heat Engines" lab was full of old heavy stationary engines which were originally used for factory and mill work. They were a perfect learning tool because they are not modified with the various bells and whistles of modern motors and engines. Seeing one of these installed in its original environment was cool.
