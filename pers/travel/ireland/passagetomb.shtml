> Passage Tomb

This passage tomb was recently excavated. Although not clear here, the width of
the top stone is roughly 4 ft. Many other such tombs, some surrounded by stone circles were found
at this same site, Carrowmore, near Sligo Town in Northwest Ireland (Co. Sligo).

No-one is absolutely clear about how and why such tombs were built. It is clear that not everyone
got such a tomb. In the case of this ancient graveyard, this tomb was believed to be the most important,
both because of its location (it was centrally located on the highest part of the area) and the fact that
most, if not all, of the other tombs' opennings pointed to this tomb.
