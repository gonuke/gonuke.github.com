Connemara National Park
> Connemara

Here we cross the bogs in the direction of Mt. Diamond hidden in the clouds. While it may appear
to be a nice thickly carpeted meadow, this field is actually a soft sponge-like peat bog, and with every
step comes the risk of a filthy wet foot!

Connemara National Park has a lot to offer any visitor. As it happens, about 40 other French and Italian
tourists knew this. There is a small museum which includes information about the physical and human
history of the bogs. In addition, there are interpretive walks, A/V presentations, and interesting talks about
a variety of topics, including the Connemara ponies. These horses first arrived in the area following a
Spanish shipwreck of Ireland's West coast, and have developed into their own breed. Gene, our
horseback guide, has one purebred Connemara which he is hoping to breed and use in his riding
business.
