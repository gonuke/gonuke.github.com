A Night Out in Dublin

> A Night Out

For various reasons, it has been a long time since Laurie and I have really gone out on the town. Since we had to get up and drive to the Airport at 5 in the morning, this was probably not the best night to start....

We met at Oliver St. John Gogarty's, coincidentally the same place that the Musical Pub Crawl began. It turns out that it is not only a bar for tourists, but also frequented by a lot of people who live in Dublin. We stayed there for much of the evening just chatting over a few pints and meeting a few of Tracy and Shannon's friends. When closing time arrived, Shannon was missing because she had been persuaded to run off to the grand opening of the All Sports Café, just across the street. It seems that they knew a local shopowner who had been bought out by the developer in order to build this restaurant/bar. By the time we arrived, after closing time of the pubs, there was nearly no-one there. I will leave it up to you to decide whether you think it was highly exclusive or really lame!

After a couple of glasses of champagne with the All Sports Café's owners, we escaped to another bar known to locals as an after-hour bar. These are the places where Dublin residents go after the pubs are closed (about midnight) to get away from all the tourists. Tracy and Shannon were regulars, so we managed to get in with no trouble, despite our tourist-like apparel (I was wearing shorts, as always, not necessarily meeting the dresscode of many a place).

We eventually tore ourselves away, after quite a bit to drink, at 2 AM, and took a cab home. The worst part of the whole night was getting up three hours later. We both felt absolutely terrible and the flight to Edinburgh was not the most pleasant I have had :)
