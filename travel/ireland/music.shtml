The Search for Music in Ireland

> Music in Ireland

One of the things that I searched for throughout Ireland was authentic music. Having seen Riverdance in London at Christmas 1995, I was excited about the prospect of finding a small, out-of-the-way pub in a small, out-of-the-way village and hearing some real folk music being strummed, blown and beaten out by the locals. I suppose this was a romantic notion...

In Mitchelstown, we expected to find the "Mitchelstown International Music Festival", promising lots of good music, small friendly stages and good folky jamming. When it was cancelled and we were sent to Millstreet, we were disappointed to see that it cost a small fortune to watch professionally mixed big name bands on indoor and rain-soaked outdoor stages. Glengarrif sported, amongs others, a French Country and Western singer. Granted, in Kilfenora, we successfully attended the CD release Ceili for the Kilfenora Ceili band, but Ceili music wasn't what I had in mind either. In Cleggan, in a region known for its music, we searched high and low throughout all the pubs only to find one in which some kind of electronic keyboards were being set up.

In Dublin, when we finally found this "Traditional Irish Music" pub crawl, I was sure that the quest would finally be fulfilled. It was close! The two musicians which hosted our tour played interesting and beautiful music. They talked a little bit about the development of Irish music, but mostly about how they had learned the various songs from this and that musician. The tour wasn't helped by the fact that there were one or two obnoxious Canadians (no, not us!) who could not keep the beat, but insisted on clapping to the music anyway. Again, I would recommend this tour to anyone, but note that you will only hear the two guides, not a different group in each pub as we thought from the flier.
